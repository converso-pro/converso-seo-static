Understanding Attribution Models

Attribution models determine how credit for conversions is assigned to different marketing touchpoints. Choose the right model based on your sales cycle and customer journey.

First-Touch Attribution

Blog 100% Email 0% Retargeting 0%

Gives 100% credit to the first interaction. Best for understanding top-of-funnel effectiveness and brand awareness campaigns.

Last-Touch Attribution

Blog 0% Email 0% Retargeting 100%

Assigns all credit to the final touchpoint. Common in e-commerce but undervalues awareness and nurturing efforts.

Linear Attribution

Blog 33% Email 33% Retargeting 33%

Distributes credit equally across all touchpoints. Simple but treats all interactions as equally valuable.

Time-Decay Attribution

Blog 20% Email 30% Retargeting 50%

More credit to recent touchpoints. Ideal for short sales cycles where recent interactions drive conversions.

U-Shaped Attribution

Blog 40% Email 20% Retargeting 40%

Emphasizes first and last touch (40% each), with 20% to middle interactions. Balances awareness and conversion focus.

Data-Driven Attribution

Blog 35% Email 25% Retargeting 40%

Uses machine learning to assign credit based on actual impact. Most accurate but requires significant data volume.
