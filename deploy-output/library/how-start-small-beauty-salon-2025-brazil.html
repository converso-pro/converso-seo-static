How to Start a Small Beauty Salon in 2025 in Brazil?

Complete guide updated with new rules and opportunities

üìù Quick Answer

To start a small beauty salon in Brazil in 2025, you need $1,000 to $3,000 initial investment, MEI registration (free), 215-430 sq ft space (can be at home), basic equipment and online booking system like Converso.pro (free plan available).

The beauty market in Brazil moves $24 billion/year and grows 5% annually. With proper planning, you can earn $1,000 to $3,000/month working alone, or $4,000+ with a small team. The secret is starting small, focusing on profitable services (like coloring and eyebrow design) and using technology to automate bookings and payments.

üéØ Complete Step-by-Step to Open Your Salon

Open your MEI (Individual Microentrepreneur)

It's free and 100% online through Portal do Empreendedor. Use CNAE 9602-5/01 (Hairdressers, manicure and pedicure). Revenue limit: R$81,000/year ($16,200/year). Fixed tax: about $14/month. You'll have tax ID, can issue invoices and accept cards with better rates.

Choose the Ideal Location

Options: 1) At home (save $300-600/month in rent), 2) Small commercial space (215-430 sq ft), 3) Beauty coworking (pay only when you use). To start at home, check if your condo allows it. Prefer locations with easy parking and good visibility.

Buy Essential Equipment

Basic list: hydraulic chair ($160-300), portable wash basin ($120-240), large mirror ($40), auxiliary cart ($30), professional hair dryer ($40-80), flat iron and curling iron ($60), manicure kit ($40). Total: $500-800. Buy used on classified ads to save 50%.

Regularize with Health Surveillance

Required: sanitary permit from city hall ($20-60), online biosafety course ($10-20), sterilization kit (autoclave or dry heat sterilizer - $80-160). Some cities have simplified rules for MEI. Check with local health surveillance.

Set Up Online Booking System

Use Converso.pro: create your professional website in 5 minutes, accept 24/7 bookings, send automatic WhatsApp reminders, receive online payments. Free plan allows 10 bookings/month, ideal to start. This eliminates phone calls and reduces no-shows by 40%.

Define Services and Competitive Prices

Research 5 nearby salons. Start with prices 20% below to attract clients. Most profitable services: coloring (300% margin), eyebrow design (500% margin), eyelash extensions (400% margin). Create combos: blowout + treatment with 15% discount.

Promote and Win First Clients

Free strategies: 1) Offer 50% discount to first 10 clients in exchange for photos and testimonials, 2) Create professional Instagram with before/after, 3) Join local Facebook groups, 4) Partner with local influencers (exchange for services), 5) Referral program: who brings friend gets 20% discount.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Can I start a salon at home?

Yes! Many successful salons started at home. Check your condo rules and have a separate space for service. Health surveillance is usually more flexible with residential MEI. Save on rent and invest in quality.

How long to recover investment?

With 5-6 services/day at $16 each, you earn $1,760-2,112/month. Deducting costs (40%), you keep $1,056-1,267. $1,000 investment pays itself in 1-2 months. Well-managed salons recover investment in 30-60 days.

Do I need employees to start?

No! Start alone to understand the business and build your clientele. When you have full schedule (30+ clients/week), consider an assistant or partner manicurist. Many profitable salons operate with just 1-2 professionals.

Which courses are required?

Required: biosafety course (online, $10-20). Recommended: colorimetry, eyebrow design, eyelash extensions. Many online courses cost $40-100. Technical schools offer in-person courses with recognized certificates.

How to compete with big salons?

Your differential: personalized service, flexible hours (work Saturday night, Sunday), fair price, cozy atmosphere. Clients value proximity and exclusive attention. 73% prefer small salons for personalized experience.
