💡 Tips to Maximize Your Earnings

1. Specialize in Lucrative Niches

Focus on barbershops, beauty salons, repair shops, and medical offices. These businesses need online booking and pay well for professional solutions. A barbershop website can easily cost $3,000-5,000.

2. Offer Complete Packages

Don't just sell the website. Include: Google My Business setup, WhatsApp integration, training, initial content creation, and 3 months of support. This justifies higher prices.

3. Build Recurring Income

With Converso.pro's 30% commission, each client generates passive income. 50 clients paying $18/month = $270/month recurring commission. In 2 years, you can have $1,000+/month guaranteed.

4. Use Templates as a Base

Create 3-4 custom templates for your main niches. This reduces development time from 30 days to 3 days, allowing you to serve more clients with the same quality.

5. Automate Everything Possible

Use tools like Zapier to automate: sending proposals, client onboarding, payment reminders. This frees up your time to focus on sales and development.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know programming to use Converso.pro?

Not necessarily. The platform has ready templates and a visual editor. But basic HTML/CSS knowledge helps for customizations. Developers can create more professional websites and charge more.

How long does it take to recover the investment in tools?

With just 1 project of $2,000, you cover all monthly tool costs (about $100/month) and still profit $1,900. Most developers recover their investment with their first client.

Can I work with international clients?

Yes! Converso.pro supports 3 languages (PT, EN, ES) and multiple currencies. With Stripe or PayPal, you can receive from any country. Brazilian developers charge $500-2000 to American clients for the same work.

Is it worth investing in paid tools from the start?

Start with the free ones (VS Code, Git, Figma) and invest first in Converso.pro, which is your money-making tool. As you grow, add the others. The important thing is to start and generate revenue.
