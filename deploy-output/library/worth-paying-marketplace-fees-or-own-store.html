Is it worth paying marketplace fees or having your own store?

Complete analysis comparing costs, advantages, and when each option is more profitable

üí° Quick Answer

It depends on your monthly revenue:

  • Up to $500/month: Marketplaces are more advantageous
  • $501 to $1,500/month: Consider having both
  • Above $1,500/month: Own store is more profitable

With sales of $3,000/month, you save $420/month having your own store instead of paying 15% commission.

üéØ Definitive Decision Guide

üìå Use ONLY Marketplace if:

  • Just starting out (less than 6 months)
  • Revenue less than $500/month
  • No time for marketing
  • Selling generic products/commodities
  • Want to validate product before investing

‚ú® Use BOTH (Hybrid Strategy) if:

  • Revenue between $500 and $3,000/month
  • Want to diversify sales channels
  • Have different products for each channel
  • Can manage two channels
  • Seeking accelerated growth

üöÄ Use ONLY Own Store if:

  • Revenue over $3,000/month
  • Have strong/differentiated brand
  • Want to build a real business
  • Have marketing strategy
  • Seeking long-term growth

üîÑ How to Migrate from Marketplace to Own Store

üìÖ 90-Day Timeline:

‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: Don't abandon marketplace at once!

Make a gradual transition to avoid losing revenue.

Month 1: Preparation

  • Choose and set up your platform
  • Import products and organize categories
  • Configure payment methods
  • Create institutional pages

  • Add exclusive products to store
  • Monitor metrics and optimize

üí° Expert Final Tips

  1. Start with both: Use marketplace for cash flow while building your store
  2. Differentiate products: Premium products in store, commodities on marketplace
  3. Always collect data: Email and phone from all customers
  4. Invest in remarketing: Customer who bought once, buys again
  5. Calculate LTV: Customer lifetime value justifies having own store
  6. Automate processes: Use tools to manage both channels
  7. Monitor competition: Adjust prices and strategies constantly
