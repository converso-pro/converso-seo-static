‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions about Pricing

Client thinks $1,000 is expensive. How to justify?

Show the ROI: With 2 more clients per week (thanks to online booking), the system pays for itself in 2 months. After that, it's pure profit. Calculate: 8 clients/month x $50 average ticket = $400/month extra.

Should I charge monthly maintenance?

Yes! Charge $40 to $100/month for "management and optimization". Include: content updates, monthly reports, priority support. With white label platform, you have no additional cost.

What if competitor charges $400?

Differentiate by value, not price. Your website has: professional booking, zero system fees, included support, automatic updates. Cheap becomes expensive when the system stops working.

Can I offer payment plans to the client?

Yes, but with 50% down payment. Example: $1,200 = $600 down + 3x $200. This ensures your initial profit and client commitment.

How to price for franchises or multi-locations?

Use scalable model: First location $1,600, additional $600/location. For 5 locations: $1,600 + (4 x $600) = $4,000. Saves 40% vs individual.

üéØ Golden Pricing Tips

  1. Never mention "template" or "ready-made" - Talk about "custom solution" and "professional system"
  2. Present 3 price options - Basic, Recommended (highlighted), Premium
  3. Include "bonuses" at no extra cost - Google My Business, basic SEO, training
  4. Use price anchoring - "Traditional development would cost $3,000..."
  5. Offer guarantee - "30 days of free adjustments" or "Satisfaction guaranteed"
  6. Create urgency - "Price valid for this week only"
  7. Show success stories - "See how Barbershop X increased revenue by 40%"
