What is the best payment gateway for e-commerce in Brazil in 2025?

Honest and complete analysis of Brazil's 8 main payment gateways

💡 Quick Answer

There's no single "best" gateway. The choice depends on your business:

  • Beginners (up to $2k/month): PagSeguro or Mercado Pago
  • Growth ($2k-20k/month): Stripe or Pagar.me
  • High volume ($20k+/month): Cielo or Rede (negotiate rates)
  • International: PayPal + Stripe

Pro Tip: Always have 2 gateways to never lose sales if one goes down!

💸 Hidden Costs Nobody Talks About

⚠️ Watch Out for Extra Costs!

Beyond basic fees, consider:

  • Chargeback: $3 to $10 per dispute (even if you win)
  • Monthly fee: Some charge $10-40/month above X transactions
  • Inactivity: Fee if no payments processed for 90 days
  • Early settlement: 0.5% to 1% additional
  • Withdrawal fee: $0.60 to $2 per transfer
  • Currency conversion: 4% to 6% on international transactions
  • Refund: You lose the fee even with cancellation
  • Manual review: $0.14 per transaction in some cases

✅ Market Best Practices

  1. Always use 2 gateways: Main + backup (avoid losing sales)
  2. Negotiate after 6 months: With history, get better rates
  3. Monitor chargebacks: Above 1% can cause problems
  4. Test before launch: Simulate all possible scenarios
  5. Configure webhooks: Essential for reliable automation
  6. Document everything: Screenshots help in disputes
  7. Enable antifraud: Better to reject than have chargeback
  8. Offer PIX: Lower fee and instant money

🔄 How to Migrate Gateways (Without Losing Sales)

Safe Step-by-Step:

  1. Implement new in parallel: Don't turn off the old one yet
  2. Test with 10% of traffic: Use A/B test for 15 days
  3. Migrate subscriptions gradually: 20% per week
  4. Keep history: Export all data first
  5. Notify saved customers: Email about new checkout
  6. Monitor metrics: Conversion rate must maintain
  7. Have rollback ready: If something goes wrong, revert quickly
