🛠️ Implementation Guide for Personal Trainers

Day 1: Platform Setup

Create your trainer profile, upload professional photos, and configure basic business information. Set your brand colors and customize your booking page.

Day 1: Service Configuration

Add all training services with descriptions, durations, and pricing. Include one-on-one, group sessions, assessments, and any specialty services you offer.

Day 2: Schedule & Availability

Set your working hours, break times, and days off. Configure travel time between locations if you train at multiple gyms or clients' homes.

Day 2: Payment Processing

Set up payment methods, configure package deals, and establish cancellation policies. Consider requiring deposits for new clients.

Day 3: Client Communication

Configure automated reminders, confirmation messages, and follow-up sequences. Set up intake forms for new client health and fitness assessments.

Day 3: Integration & Testing

Connect with your existing tools (fitness apps, nutrition tracking, etc.), test the entire booking flow, and train on the admin interface.

Launch: Client Migration

Introduce the system to existing clients, share your booking link on social media, and add it to your email signature and business cards.

💡 Best Practices for Trainer Booking Systems

📱 Mobile-First Design

Most clients book on mobile devices. Ensure your booking system works perfectly on smartphones and is easy to navigate during gym visits.

💳 Flexible Payment Options

Offer session packages, monthly memberships, and pay-per-session options. Require deposits for new clients to reduce no-shows.

📋 Client Health Forms

Use digital intake forms to collect health history, fitness goals, and preferences before the first session. Saves time and improves safety.

⏰ Smart Scheduling

Set buffer times between sessions, block travel time between locations, and allow same-day cancellations for flexibility while protecting your revenue.
