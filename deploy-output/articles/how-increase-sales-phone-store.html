⚠️ 7 Mistakes That Kill Sales (and How to Avoid)

1. Focusing only on low price

Customer buys value, not price. Highlight benefits: warranty, support, authenticity, delivery. Who competes only on price, breaks.

2. Salesperson who doesn't know products

Train team weekly. Salesperson needs to know technical differences, compatibilities, advantages. Knowledge generates trust.

3. Disorganized and dirty store

Clean showcase, products organized by category, visible prices. Customer decides in 7 seconds whether to enter or not. First impression sells.

4. Not offering installments

Offer up to 3x interest-free on cash/transfer. Use card machine for 6-12x. Facilitate purchase, customer spends more.

5. Ignoring after-sales

Call after 7 days: "Everything ok with your phone?". Remembered customer buys 3x more. After-sales retains.

6. No digital presence

70% research online before going to physical store. Without Instagram and Google My Business, you don't exist.

7. Anxious and invasive salesperson

Greet and say "Feel free to browse, I'm here if you need". Let customer explore. Pressure repels, consultation attracts.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How to compete with big chains and online?

Your edge is proximity and agility. Offer: free screen protector installation, phone setup, 24/7 WhatsApp support, 2-hour delivery. Big chains don't do this.

What's the ideal margin for accessories?

Work with minimum 50% margin on basic accessories (cases, screen protectors) and 35-40% on electronics (earphones, chargers). Volume compensates lower margin.

Is it worth selling used phones?

Yes! Margin can reach 40%. Accept as trade-in for new, resell with 90-day warranty. Use system to track origin.

How to make customer return?

Create repurchase calendar: screen protector (3 months), case (6 months), earphones (1 year). Send reminder with exclusive discount. Customer likes to be reminded when they need.

How many bundles should I create?

Start with 5 bundles focused on main profiles: executive, gamer, driver, fitness, basic. Analyze sales monthly and adjust. Fewer options, easier decision.
