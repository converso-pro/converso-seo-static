Growth Hacking Framework

1

Define North Star Metric

Identify the single metric that best captures the value you deliver to customers. This could be daily active users, revenue per user, or customer lifetime value. Every growth experiment should ultimately impact this metric.

2

Rapid Experimentation

Run 10-20 experiments per week with small tests. Use the ICE framework (Impact, Confidence, Ease) to prioritize. Document every experiment with hypothesis, method, and results.

3

Data-Driven Decisions

Set up comprehensive analytics to track user behavior. Use cohort analysis, funnel visualization, and A/B testing to validate assumptions. Let data guide your strategy, not opinions.

4

Scale What Works

When you find tactics that move your North Star Metric, double down. Allocate more resources, automate processes, and optimize for maximum impact. Kill experiments that don't show results quickly.

5

Build Growth Loops

Create self-reinforcing cycles where user actions drive more user acquisition. The best growth hacks become product features that continuously drive growth without ongoing effort.
